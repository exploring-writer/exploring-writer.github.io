Cooking Guidelines

Healthy Cooking Methods

Preferred Methods

  • Steaming
    • Best for preserving nutrients
    • No added fats needed
    • Ideal for vegetables and fish
  • Boiling
    • Simple and healthy
    • Good for soups and stews
    • Tip: Don't overcook vegetables to preserve nutrients

Methods to Minimize

  • Deep Frying
    • Adds unnecessary calories and fats
    • Can create harmful compounds at high temperatures
  • Grilling
    • Can create carcinogens when food is charred
    • If grilling, avoid burning and use marinades

Healthy Diet Styles

MIND Diet

  • Combines Mediterranean and DASH diets
  • Focus on:
    • Green leafy vegetables
    • Berries
    • Whole grains
    • Fish
    • Olive oil

Mediterranean Diet

  • Plant-based foods
  • Healthy fats (olive oil)
  • Herbs and spices instead of salt
  • Fish and poultry over red meat

Herbs & Spices

  • Common Mediterranean herbs:
    • Basil, oregano, thyme, rosemary
    • Parsley, sage, mint
  • Healthy spices:
    • Turmeric, ginger, garlic
    • Cinnamon, black pepper

Digestive Health Considerations

Temperature Control

  • Keep food temperature below 65°C
    • Protects esophagus and stomach
    • Reduces risk of thermal injury

Food Freshness

  • Use fresh ingredients
  • Store properly to maintain freshness
  • Check expiration dates

Avoid Irritants

  • Limit:
    • Extremely spicy foods
    • Very acidic/sour foods
    • Excessive salt

Practical Tips

  • Meal Preparation:
    • Plan meals in advance
    • Prep ingredients before cooking
    • Use proper portion sizes
  • Seasoning:
    • Use herbs and spices liberally
    • Reduce salt gradually to adjust taste
    • Add salt at the end of cooking
  • Equipment:
    • Use a steamer basket
    • Non-stick pans to reduce oil use
    • Food thermometer for safe temperatures
