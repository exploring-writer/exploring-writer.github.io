Health Guidelines

Core Principles Summary

  • Regularity - Wake-up, bedtime, and meal schedules
  • Eating Habits - Balanced, moderate portions, plant-based diet, avoid extreme diets that may damage organs
  • Exercise, Rest, Hygiene, Regular Check-ups, Stress Management - Review your habits
  • Not perfect habits, but sustainable regular habits

Important Notes

  • This guide is compiled from various sources including university hospitals, major hospitals (Korean, US), government resources, and EBS documentaries. The numbers provided should be used as general reference points rather than strict rules, as optimal values vary by individual and source.
  • Some content may not be suitable for those with specific medical conditions. Consult with your doctor.
  • I am not a medical professional. Feedback and corrections are welcome.

Obstacles to Goals

  1. Early failure of a vital organ (due to arteriosclerosis, cancer, poor lifestyle habits) ‚Üí Death
  2. Pelvic fracture ‚Üí Bleeding, organ damage, long-term bed rest ‚Üí Higher probability of death from complications (especially with age)
  3. Sudden fatal accidents or infectious diseases

While #3 is unfortunate, good lifestyle habits can significantly reduce the risk of #1 and improve #2 to some extent.

Good lifestyle habits are more effective when started younger. So start now!

Areas for Improvement Through Good Lifestyle Habits

Long-term Health

  • Physical age ~ Can be viewed as organ wear and tear
    • Factors: Aging degree ~ Total insulin secreted
  • Reduce organ load
    • Example: Less insulin secretion - Regular, intermittent, moderate meals
    • Quit smoking, moderate drinking, rest
  • Organ DNA damage & repair
    • If DNA repair doesn't occur properly ‚Üí Aging or cancer
    • üôÇ Help repair: Autophagy (occurs when hungry), regular biological cycle, sufficient sleep
    • ü§¢ Damage or hinder repair: Stress, poor hygiene, bad air

Vascular Health

  • High blood pressure & high blood viscosity
    • Blocks and ruptures some brain and organ blood vessels ‚Üí Brain damage, organ damage
  • Prevention
    • Manage triglycerides, cholesterol: Avoid high carbs, saturated fats, trans fats
    • Manage high blood pressure

Bone Health

  • Strengthen bones & joints: Calcium, jogging (provides gentle impact between bones and discs for better nutrient absorption)
  • Prevent falls & minimize injuries when falling: Balance exercises, strength training

Mental Health

  • Mind is part of body, brain and body interact through chemicals ‚Üí Will is not free
  • Mental health ‚Üî Physical health
    • Ignoring mental health threatens physical health. Mental health is also a regular health management item.
    • Impact of Stress
      • Stress ‚Üí Cortisol decrease ‚Üí Serotonin secretion inhibition ‚Üí Depression
      • Adrenal glands (organs regulating homeostasis) exhaust with each stress ‚Üí Immune system decline, fatigue, various burnout symptoms
      • Gut and brain are connected: Gastritis from stress
      • Stress lowers pain threshold ‚Üí Pain from minor things
  • Cognition/Dementia prevention/Memory
    • Need to stimulate brain through mental activity
  • Limit social media

Skin Health

  • Various skin conditions (atopic dermatitis, eczema, psoriasis, folliculitis, seborrheic dermatitis, lichen, etc.) are inflammatory responses
    • May improve with overall health (immunity) improvement
    • Reduce body inflammation
      • Decreases with reduction in abdominal fat (waist circumference)
