Cardio Training Guidelines

Maximum Heart Rate = 209 - 0.7 × Age

The examples below are for someone with a maximum heart rate of 184.

  • General Guidelines
    • If you're not out of breath at all, no matter how long you do it, it's not exercise
  • Moderate Intensity
    • Moderate intensity aerobic exercise: Optimal for weight loss
    • 64-76% of maximum heart rate (118-140)
      • Some sources say 60-70% (110-129), probably for beginners?
    • 150-200 minutes/week
  • High Intensity
    • High intensity aerobic exercise: Optimal for improving cardiorespiratory endurance
    • 77% or more of maximum heart rate (142-171)
    • 75-100 minutes/week
    • If more than 150 minutes, mortality rate actually increases
  • HIIT and Conditioning Exercises
    • Afterburn effect
      • Can last up to 38 hours
    • High intensity
      • No need to reach maximum heart rate within 1 minute. That's too strenuous.
      • 80% or more of maximum heart rate (>147)
    • Rest
      • Heart rate: 100-120
      • 1-2 minutes to catch your breath (until it drops below 120), 3 minutes is sufficient
    • Aerobic
      • High intensity 0.5-1 minute → Moderate intensity 1-2 minutes, 7-10 sets
    • Anaerobic
      • 30-60 minutes (if too long, stress hormones are released)
      • Fast and heavy
      • Single-joint and machine exercises not recommended
      • Tabata
        • 20 seconds maximum speed → 10 seconds rest, 8 sets
  • Other Guidelines
    • Aerobic exercise must last at least 10 minutes, 15 minutes is effective
    • Reach high intensity heart rate → Rest for 1 minute → Repeat
