Types of Stretching

Dynamic Stretching

  • Description:
    • Active movement through full range of motion
    • Controlled, smooth movements
    • Most effective for improving flexibility
  • Examples:
    • Arm circles
    • Leg swings
    • Walking lunges
    • Hip circles

Static Stretching

  • Description:
    • Hold a stretch position for 15-30 seconds
    • No movement during the stretch
    • Best performed after exercise
  • Examples:
    • Hamstring stretch
    • Quadriceps stretch
    • Shoulder stretch

Passive Stretching

  • Description:
    • External force assists the stretch
    • Partner or equipment helps achieve deeper stretch
    • Relaxed muscles during stretch
  • Examples:
    • Partner-assisted stretches
    • Using resistance bands
    • Using walls or furniture for support

Active Stretching

  • Description:
    • Using own muscle strength
    • No external assistance
    • Builds strength and flexibility simultaneously
  • Examples:
    • Holding leg raises
    • Yoga poses
    • Ballet positions

Proper Sequence

Recommended Order

  1. Warm-up (5-10 minutes):
    • Light cardio to raise body temperature
    • Walking, jogging, or jumping jacks
  2. Dynamic stretching:
    • Prepare muscles for activity
    • Enhance range of motion
  3. Main activity/exercise
  4. Static/passive stretching:
    • Cool down period
    • Enhance recovery

Important Safety Considerations

  • Always warm up before stretching to prevent injury
  • Don't bounce or force stretches
  • Stop if you feel pain (not to be confused with mild discomfort)
  • Breathe steadily and regularly during stretches
  • Maintain proper form and alignment

Best Practices

  • Frequency:
    • Stretch daily for best results
    • Focus on major muscle groups
    • Hold stretches for appropriate duration
  • Progress gradually:
    • Start with basic stretches
    • Increase intensity and duration over time
    • Listen to your body's signals
