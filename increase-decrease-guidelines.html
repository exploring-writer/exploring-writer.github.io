Nutrients to Increase & Decrease

Nutrients to Increase

Calcium

  • Sources:
    • Dairy products (milk, yogurt, cheese)
    • Leafy greens (kale, collard greens)
    • Fish with bones (sardines, salmon)
    • Fortified foods (plant milk, tofu)
    • Nuts and seeds (almonds, chia seeds)
  • Benefits:
    • Bone and teeth health
    • Muscle function
    • Nerve signaling

Folate (Vitamin B9)

  • Sources:
    • Dark green vegetables (spinach, broccoli)
    • Legumes (lentils, beans)
    • Citrus fruits
    • Whole grains
    • Fortified cereals
  • Benefits:
    • Cell growth and DNA synthesis
    • Red blood cell formation
    • Neural tube development (crucial during pregnancy)

Nutrients to Decrease

Salt and Sodium

  • Daily Limits:
    • Salt: Less than 5g per day
    • Sodium: Less than 2g per day
  • Hidden Sources:
    • Processed foods
    • Restaurant meals
    • Canned foods
    • Condiments and sauces
    • Bread and cereals
  • Health Impact of Excess:
    • High blood pressure
    • Increased heart disease risk
    • Kidney strain
    • Water retention

Balanced Nutrition Through Key Foods

Core Food Groups for Nutrient Sufficiency

  • Legumes:
    • Protein, iron, zinc
    • Fiber, B vitamins
  • Cruciferous Vegetables:
    • Vitamin C, K
    • Folate, fiber
    • Antioxidants
  • Nuts:
    • Healthy fats
    • Protein, minerals
    • Vitamin E
  • Whole Grains:
    • B vitamins
    • Fiber, minerals
    • Plant protein
  • Fish:
    • Omega-3 fatty acids
    • High-quality protein
    • Vitamin D, B12

Practical Tips

  • Reducing Salt:
    • Use herbs and spices instead
    • Read nutrition labels
    • Cook at home more often
    • Rinse canned foods
  • Increasing Essential Nutrients:
    • Eat a variety of colorful foods
    • Include protein-rich foods at each meal
    • Choose whole grains over refined grains
    • Include leafy greens daily
