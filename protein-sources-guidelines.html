Protein Sources Guidelines

Quality Criteria for Protein Sources

Three Main Factors:

  1. Fat Content
    • Consider saturated fat content when meeting protein requirements
    • Some sources (like red meat) come with high saturated fat
    • Better sources have healthy fats (e.g., omega-3 in fish)
  2. Complete Protein Status
    • Complete proteins contain all 20 essential amino acids
    • Animal sources: All are complete proteins
    • Plant sources: Most are incomplete, but some exceptions exist
  3. Environmental Impact
    • Livestock industry is a major contributor to climate change
    • Reducing meat consumption has more impact than many other eco-friendly actions

Protein Source Rankings

Plant Proteins > Fish = Poultry > Dairy >> Pork > Beef/Lamb > Processed Meat

Best Sources: Plant Proteins

Plant-Based Proteins (식물성 단백질)

  • Benefits:
    • Best protein value for health
    • Rich in micronutrients and fiber
    • Lowest environmental impact
    • Contains healthy fats
    • Good absorption rate
  • Complete protein sources:
    • Tofu (두부)
    • Quinoa (퀴노아)
  • Complementary proteins:
    • Rice + Beans = Complete protein
    • Need 10% more intake compared to animal proteins

Good Sources: Fish and Poultry

1. Fish (생선)

  • Benefits:
    • Contains healthy omega-3 fats
    • Note: Omega-3 supplements don't show the same health benefits
  • Recommendations:
    • 2-3 servings of small fish per week
    • Best choices (low heavy metal content):
      • Anchovies (멸치)
      • Mackerel (고등어) - Korean/Norwegian
      • Other small fish lower in food chain

2. Poultry (가금류)

  • Benefits:
    • Very low in saturated fat (without skin)
    • Complete protein source
    • Lower environmental impact than red meat

Moderate Sources: Dairy

  • Benefits:
    • Good source of calcium
    • Complete protein
  • Recommendations:
    • Prefer yogurt over milk (fermented, better for gut health)

Limited Sources: Red and Processed Meats

Red Meat (적색육) and Processed Meats (가공육)

  • Concerns:
    • High in saturated fat
    • Highest environmental impact
    • Beef and lamb have highest carbon emissions
  • Recommendation:
    • Minimize consumption for both health and environmental reasons

Practical Tips

Daily Protein Optimization:

  • Fish consumption:
    • 2-3 times per week (mainly mackerel)
    • Occasional sashimi and seafood
  • Increase plant protein:
    • 3 times per week: Half block tofu + 1 egg
    • Add beans to rice
  • Daily yogurt as snack
  • Replace full chicken breast with half + lentils
  • Chickpeas as snacks

Per meal guideline: 25g protein with minimal saturated fat and environmental impact
