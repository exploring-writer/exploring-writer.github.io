Eye Health

Myopia Prevention

  • Distance Vision Practice:
    • Look at objects 20+ feet away for 20 seconds every 20 minutes (20-20-20 rule)
    • Spend at least 2 hours outdoors daily
    • Focus on distant landscapes, mountains, or buildings
  • Natural Light Benefits:
    • Natural light helps regulate eye development
    • Reduces eye strain compared to artificial light
    • Helps maintain proper circadian rhythm

UV Protection

Skin Protection

  • Sunscreen Application:
    • Apply SPF 30+ sunscreen 15-30 minutes before sun exposure
    • Reapply every 2 hours or after swimming/sweating
    • Use broad-spectrum protection (UVA + UVB)
  • Protective Clothing:
    • Wear wide-brimmed hats
    • Choose UV-protective clothing
    • Cover exposed areas during peak sun hours (10 AM - 4 PM)

Eye Protection

  • Sunglasses:
    • Use UV400 rated sunglasses
    • Wear wrap-around styles for better protection
    • Use polarized lenses to reduce glare
  • Additional Measures:
    • Wear a hat with a brim
    • Seek shade during peak UV hours
    • Be extra careful near reflective surfaces (water, snow)

Hair Protection

  • Prevention:
    • Use UV-protective hair products
    • Wear a hat or use hair coverings
    • Avoid prolonged sun exposure during peak hours
  • Care:
    • Use deep conditioning treatments
    • Minimize heat styling on sun-exposed hair
    • Consider protective hairstyles

Morning Sunlight

Timing and Benefits

  • Optimal Exposure:
    • Get 10-30 minutes of morning sun (before 10 AM)
    • Expose face and arms if temperature permits
    • Maintain consistent daily timing
  • Health Benefits:
    • Regulates circadian rhythm
    • Improves sleep quality
    • Enhances vitamin D production
    • Boosts mood and energy levels

Important Safety Considerations

  • UV Index Awareness:
    • Check daily UV index
    • Take extra precautions when UV index is high (6+)
    • Limit exposure during peak hours
  • Medical Conditions:
    • Consult healthcare provider if photosensitive
    • Be aware of medications that increase sun sensitivity
    • Take extra precautions if you have eye conditions

Best Practices

  • Daily Routine:
    • Start with morning sunlight exposure
    • Practice distance viewing throughout the day
    • Maintain consistent outdoor activity schedule
  • Protection Strategy:
    • Layer protection (sunscreen, clothing, shade)
    • Keep UV protection items easily accessible
    • Set reminders for sunscreen reapplication
