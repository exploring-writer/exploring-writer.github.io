Fat Sources Guidelines

Key Principle

Focus on reducing rather than increasing fat intake. Fat is naturally present in many dishes (through cooking oils and ingredients), so you'll get enough even while trying to minimize it.

Good Fats: Unsaturated Fats

1. Polyunsaturated Fats

  • Omega-3 (Most Important)
    • Best sources:
      • Fish/seafood (2-3 times per week)
        • Note: Avoid large fish high in food chain (e.g., tuna) due to mercury content
      • Flaxseed
      • Chia seeds
      • Walnuts
      • Perilla oil
  • Omega-6
    • Usually sufficient from cooking oils
    • Sources:
      • Grape seed oil
      • Soybean oil
      • Corn oil
      • Sunflower oil
      • Flaxseed

2. Monounsaturated Fats (Omega-9)

  • Sources:
    • Olive oil
    • Canola oil
    • Sesame oil
    • Avocado
    • Nuts and seeds:
      • Almonds
      • Hazelnuts
      • Pecans
      • Pumpkin seeds
      • Sesame seeds

Fats to Minimize

1. Saturated Fats

  • Common sources:
    • Red meat (beef, pork, lamb)
    • Dairy products:
      • Butter
      • Cheese
      • Ice cream
      • Full-fat milk/yogurt
    • Some baked goods
    • Mayonnaise
    • Coconut products
  • Tips:
    • Choose low-fat dairy products
    • Check fat and calorie content before purchasing

2. Trans Fats (Avoid Completely)

  • Common in franchise foods (improves taste/texture and shelf life)
  • Sources to avoid:
    • Margarine
    • Shortening (check labels on bread/cookies)
    • Hydrogenated oils (in restaurant fried foods)
    • Coffee creamers

3. Cholesterol

  • Note: Relationship between dietary cholesterol and blood cholesterol is unclear
  • High cholesterol foods:
    • Egg yolks (limit to 1 per day)
    • Shrimp
  • High carbohydrate intake may have more impact on cholesterol/triglycerides than dietary cholesterol

Practical Tips

Daily Fat Management (35g target):

  • Cooking oil awareness:
    • 1 tablespoon = ~15g fat
    • Use less than 1 teaspoon per stir-fry
  • Weekly schedule:
    • 2-3 times: Small fish (mackerel, anchovies)
    • Daily: Nuts, chia seeds (in yogurt), or nut butter
  • Cooking methods:
    • Prefer steaming/boiling over frying/grilling
    • Minimize oily foods
  • Healthy alternatives:
    • When craving fatty foods, try avocado
